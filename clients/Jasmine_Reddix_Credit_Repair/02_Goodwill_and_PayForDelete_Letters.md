## 1. Goodwill Deletion Letter (For Late Payments)
*Use this for open accounts where the client missed a payment but wants to maintain a good relationship and have the late mark removed.*

**[DATE]**

**Jasmine U. Reddix**
[Client Address]
[Client City, State, ZIP]

**[Creditor Name]**
[Creditor Address]
[Creditor City, State, ZIP]

**RE: Account #[Insert Account Number]**

Dear [Creditor Name],

I am writing to you today regarding my account [Insert Account Number]. I have been a loyal customer since [Year Account Opened] and value my relationship with [Creditor Name].

Recently, I noticed a [30/60/90]-day late payment reported on my credit file for the billing cycle of [Month, Year]. I am writing to ask if you would consider removing this late payment from my credit report as a gesture of goodwill.

During that time, I experienced a significant hardship [Briefly explain hardship: e.g., unexpected medical emergency, family crisis, temporary job loss]. This was an isolated incident and does not reflect my usual payment habits. As you can see from my history, I have always strived to maintain my account in good standing.

Since that incident, I have resolved the issue and set up automatic payments to ensure this never happens again. I am currently working hard to improve my credit score to qualify for [e.g., a mortgage, a business loan], and this single late mark is severely impacting my progress.

I respectfully request that you make a goodwill adjustment and update my credit file with Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion to remove this late payment notation. I would greatly appreciate your understanding and assistance in this matter.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,

*(Signature)*

## 2. Pay-for-Delete Negotiation Letter (For Collections)
*Use this for accounts that have been charged off or sent to collections. The goal is to pay a negotiated amount ONLY if they agree in writing to delete the negative tradeline from the credit report.*

**[DATE]**

**Jasmine U. Reddix**
[Client Address]
[Client City, State, ZIP]

**[Collection Agency Name]**
[Collection Agency Address]
[Collection Agency City, State, ZIP]

**RE: Account #[Insert Collection Account Number]**

Dear Collection Manager,

I am writing regarding the collection account listed above, which is currently appearing on my Experian credit report.

This letter is an offer to settle the alleged debt in exchange for the complete deletion of this account from all three major credit reporting agencies (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion). This is not an acknowledgment of liability for this debt in any form.

I am willing to pay **$[Insert Amount, e.g., 40% of total balance]** as full and final settlement of this account. This payment will be made via cashier's check or money order.

In exchange for this payment, [Collection Agency Name] must agree to the following terms:
1. Accept this payment as full satisfaction of the debt.
2. Completely delete all references to this account from my credit profile with Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion within 15 days of receiving payment.
3. Agree not to sell, transfer, or assign any remaining balance to another collection agency.

If you agree to these terms, please send a signed letter on your company letterhead confirming this agreement. Once I receive your written confirmation, I will immediately forward the payment of $[Insert Amount].

If I do not receive your written agreement within 15 days of the date of this letter, I will withdraw this offer and request full validation of the debt under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA).

I look forward to resolving this matter quickly.

Sincerely,

*(Signature)*

**Jasmine U. Reddix**
